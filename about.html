TriMet Rail Operations Optimization Technology (ROOT)

Rail Operations Optimization Technology, or ROOT, will use the Internet of Things and Big Data Analytics to optimize rail operations at TriMet. This page serves as a concept overview of ROOT.

Click the buttons below to learn more about ROOT’s impact on the core areas of safety and security, maintenance and on-time performance. In addition, ROOT can serve as a gateway for expanding customer information, transit signal priority and infotainment.

Safety and Security

Problem Statement


Safety: Interaction between LRT and Heavy Rail, train automation and network expansion have all increased Wayside Rail Worker Safety (WRWS) issues.


Security: Current state provides insufficient situational awareness of potential security situations on bus. Unable to provide relevant real-time visibility to security, police, or emergency responders.



Description


The ROOT concept architecture supports interfaces with 3rd party WRWS systems and with on-board CCTV cameras with on-demand streaming and distribution capability. Access to streaming CCTV video feeds will improve the response capability of police or emergency responders to safety and security incidents on the vehicle.



Alternatives


• Do nothing

• One-off proprietary system procurements

• ROOT project

Maintenance

Problem Statement


Vehicle maintenance resulting from in-service failures remains a significant fleet cost driver. Lack of insight into vehicle systems status results in suboptimal planning of maintenance activities.



Solution


The ROOT project will provide a means to perform historical and real-time reporting of vehicle systems conditions across the fleet. This will give Vehicle Maintenance rapid insights into vehicle health and allow them and dispatch to make proactive decisions. The use of Big Data will provide insights into vehicle health correlations that otherwise would go unnoticed.



Alternatives


• Do nothing

• One-off procurement

• Proprietary procurement with Siemens

• ROOT project

Customer Information

Problem Statement


Transit Tracker Real-time passenger information (RTPI) is highly accurate for the bus system running CAD/AVL. LRT feeds Transit Tracker from track contacts from rail control system and is far less accurate.



Solution


The ROOT project provides a GPS on the rail car with a cellular high-speed data connection. This architecture enables a highly accurate link for Transit Tracker to provide real-time customer information.



Alternatives


• Do nothing

• CAD/AVL Expansion to LRT Fleet

• One-off procurement

• ROOT project

Transit Signal Priority

Problem Statement


Current LRT Transit Signal Priority is largely based on trains passing trackside contact points. This provides little advanced warning to the signal and no additional data to the signal and may not be triggered if operators stay behind the trigger location.



Description


ROOT Concept Architecture supports integration of the LRT into a Connected Vehicle environment. This will allow upstream communications with signals, and the possibility of sharing additional data, like schedule and passenger load.



Alternatives


• Do nothing

• One-off procurement

• ROOT project

Infotainment

Problem Statement


Growing expectation among riders for Internet connectivity. Lack of on-board WiFi may erode rider satisfaction and deter future growth. There is no existing platform to support other Infotainment offerings.



Description


The ROOT project offers TriMet a straightforward means to provide consistent Internet access to riders, as well as, infrastructure to support future infotainment investment.

• Do nothing

• One-off procurement
